# B4 – Confirming the B2 Mechanism

To confirm the mechanism identified in B2, I would monitor the **scheduler preemption counter** (or the number of preempted sequences) because it directly indicates whether requests are being interrupted due to KV-cache pressure.

For the long-context workload, I would expect this metric to remain **0** at batch size **24**, increase to approximately **7** at batch size **32**, and further increase to approximately **23** at batch size **48**, matching the benchmark log. As preemption increases, throughput decreases even though the batch size increases, confirming that the performance bottleneck is KV-cache capacity rather than GPU compute. Therefore, the scheduler preemption counter provides direct evidence that KV-cache contention is responsible for the observed throughput anomaly.
